# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Business professionals and team leads who delegate real work to AI employees. They expect enterprise-grade reliability and trust. They are not developers — they need an interface that feels immediately competent, like handing work to a capable colleague. They arrive from enterprise sales, team invitations, or product trials.

## Product Purpose

Votrix lets businesses hire and manage AI employees through a chat-first interface. Each AI employee has a role, memory, and ongoing context. Success means users trust the AI enough to delegate meaningful work — the interface must communicate competence, not novelty.

## Brand Personality

Precise, trustworthy, warm. Votrix feels simultaneously technical and luxurious: an enterprise tool redesigned by a world-class type foundry. No excitement, no hype, no playfulness. Confidence through lightness and precision.

Three-word personality: **precise, warm, engineered**.

## Anti-references

- Dark mode with purple gradients, neon accents, glassmorphism, glowing particles
- Consumer chat app aesthetics (bubbly, colorful, emoji-heavy)
- SaaS landing page clichés (hero metrics, identical card grids, sparkline decorations)
- Playful or cute design language (rounded pills everywhere, pastel palettes, mascot characters)
- AI-tool marketing aesthetic (cyan-on-black, particle effects, "powered by AI" badges)
- Hedging language ("might help", "could assist") — the product is decisive

## Design Principles

1. **Trust through precision.** Every pixel signals competence. Sloppy spacing or inconsistent tokens erode the trust users need to delegate real work.
2. **Content is the interface.** The chat thread, employee responses, and work output are the product. Chrome exists to frame them, never to compete.
3. **Earned familiarity.** Users of Stripe, Linear, and Notion should feel at home immediately. Follow conventions where they exist. Innovate only where the AI employee model demands it.
4. **Lightness as luxury.** Weight 300 headlines, atmospheric blue-tinted shadows, conservative radii. The interface whispers authority rather than shouting it. Inspired by Stripe's signature restraint.
5. **Quiet confidence.** The interface never shouts. Transitions are fast. Elevation is chromatic, not dramatic. The product feels like it has been running reliably for years.

## Accessibility

Baseline: WCAG 2.1 AA on all interactive surfaces.
- Color contrast ratios verified, not eyeballed.
- All interactive elements keyboard-navigable with visible focus states.
- `prefers-reduced-motion` respected for every animation.
- Semantic HTML first, ARIA as supplement.
